# CFT vs AFT

In Paynopain, there are two mechanisms related to fund transfers associated with cards:

- **Credit Fund Transfer (CFT)**: the merchant sends money to the user's card through a direct credit.  
- **Account Funding Transactions (AFT)**: used to move funds **from** a card (of the user/sender) to a bank account, card, or even another person (receiver).  

Both concepts coexist on the platform, but they cover **different flow directions and use cases**, and have **different technical requirements** in terms of payload and KYC data.

---

## 1. High-level overview

### Credit Fund Transfer (CFT)

- **Direction of money**: from the merchant â†’ user's card.  
- **Typical use**:  
  - Returning a balance to a customer without linking it to a specific sale.  
  - Cashout / payout from the merchant's balance to Visa/Mastercard cards.
- **Operations**:
  1. A **payment order** is generated (`/payment`) with `operative: "PAYOUT"` and `secure: false`.  
  2. The **card credit** is made (`/payment/payout`) specifying:
     - `order_uuid` of the order.
     - `source_uuid` of the user's card (tokenized in Paynopain).  
- **Important characteristic**:  
  - The fund transfer is **final**; **it is not possible to reverse a card credit**.  

### Account Funding Transactions (AFT)

- **Direction of money**: from the user's card â†’  
  - an account of the user themselves (**ACCOUNT_TO_ACCOUNT**), or  
  - another person/receiver (**PERSON_TO_PERSON**).  
- **Typical use**:
  - Loading balance into an account (wallet, prepaid, etc.) using a card.
  - Sending money between people using a card as the source.
- **Where it is configured**:
  - Through the `extra_data.aft` object in the **payment order generation**.  
- **Dependencies**:
  - Depends on the **merchant's MCC** and the **card brand** to determine if the AFT type is supported.  

---

## 2. Technical similarities between CFT and AFT

Although they solve different problems, they share several concepts:

- **They work with Visa and Mastercard cards** (compatibility is always subject to the acquirer, MCC, etc.).  
- Both require **user profile data** in `extra_data.profile` (name, surname, email, etc.), although AFT requires more fields (such as `birthdate` and full address).  
- They rely on the same **payment order infrastructure** (`/payment`) and result notification (`url_post`).
- The business logic (whether the operation can be performed or not) depends on the **merchant's configuration**.

---

## 3. Key differences CFT vs AFT

### 3.1 Flow direction and roles

| Aspect                       | CFT                                                | AFT                                                                 |
|-----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Source of funds             | Merchant / merchant's balance                      | User's card (cardholder)                          |
| Destination of funds        | User's card                                        | Account/card/phone of the user or another person     |
| Main operation              | **Card credit** (payout)                           | **Account funding** (account or person-to-person)                   |
| User's role                 | Funds receiver                                     | Sender (and optionally receiver, if P2P)                            |

### 3.2 Endpoints and key fields

- **CFT**:
  - Payment order: `POST /payment`
    - `operative: "PAYOUT"`
    - `secure: false`  
    - `extra_data.profile` with minimum user data.
  - Card credit: `POST /payment/payout`
    - `order_uuid`
    - `source_uuid` (destination card).  

- **AFT**:
  - Configured with the `extra_data.aft` field when generating the payment order.  
  - Does not need a specific additional endpoint, but requires:
    - `extra_data.profile` (sender).
    - `extra_data.billing_address`.
    - `extra_data.aft` (AFT type and, if applicable, receiver).  

### 3.3 User data requirements

#### CFT

For CFT, the documentation requires at minimum:  

- `extra_data.profile.first_name`
- `extra_data.profile.last_name`
- `extra_data.profile.email`
- `extra_data.profile.phone.number`
- `extra_data.profile.phone.prefix`

The postal address and date of birth are not required in the example, although they may be recommended for compliance and scoring purposes.

#### AFT

For AFT, the sender's data requirements are stricter:  

- Within `extra_data.profile`:
  - `first_name` (required)
  - `last_name` (required)
  - `birthdate` (required, format `YYYY-MM-DD`)
  - `email` (recommended)  
- Within `extra_data.billing_address`:
  - `country` (required)
  - `address1` (required)
  - `city` (required)
  - `zip_code` (required)
  - `state_code` (required only if the country is `USA` or `CAN`)  

Additionally, if `type = "PERSON_TO_PERSON"`, a complete `receiver` must be provided.

## 4. AFT types and `extra_data.aft` structure

### 4.1 AFT types

The documentation defines three types:  

- `NONE`: forces AFT not to be applied.
- `ACCOUNT_TO_ACCOUNT`: the user's card moves funds to an account of the user themselves (own account funding).
- `PERSON_TO_PERSON`: the user's card sends funds to another person (requires receiver data).

If the `aft` field is not sent, Paylands may apply **AFT of type `ACCOUNT_TO_ACCOUNT` automatically** if:

- The merchant is configured for it according to their MCC.
- The user's data has been correctly included in `profile` and `billing_address`.  

### 4.2 Base structure of `extra_data.aft`

```json
"extra_data": {
  "profile": {
    "first_name": "Luis",
    "last_name": "Customer",
    "email": "luis@example.com",
    "birthdate": "1990-01-01"
  },
  "billing_address": {
    "country": "ESP",
    "address1": "123 Example Street",
    "city": "CastellĂłn",
    "zip_code": "12001",
    "state_code": "CT"
  },
  "aft": {
    "type": "PERSON_TO_PERSON",
    "receiver": {
      "first_name": "Ana",
      "last_name": "Receiver",
      "country": "ESP",
      "account_number": "4111111111111111",
      "account_type": "CARD_NUMBER"
    }
  }
}
```

### Notable fields

Within `extra_data.aft` the relevant fields are:

```json
"extra_data": {
  "profile": {
    "first_name": "Luis",
    "last_name": "Customer",
    "email": "luis@example.com",
    "birthdate": "1990-01-01"
  },
  "billing_address": {
    "country": "ESP",
    "address1": "123 Example Street",
    "city": "CastellĂłn",
    "zip_code": "12001",
    "state_code": "CT"
  },
  "aft": {
    "type": "PERSON_TO_PERSON",
    "receiver": {
      "first_name": "Ana",
      "last_name": "Receiver",
      "country": "ESP",
      "account_number": "4111111111111111",
      "account_type": "CARD_NUMBER"
    }
  }
}
```

Key fields:

- `aft.type` (required): [NONE, ACCOUNT_TO_ACCOUNT, PERSON_TO_PERSON]
- `aft.receiver` (required only for PERSON_TO_PERSON):
    - `first_name`
    - `last_name`
    - `country` (ISO 3 letters, for example ESP)
    - `account_number` (card, account, or mobile phone number)
    - `account_type` (optional): [CARD_NUMBER, ACCOUNT_NUMBER, MOBILE_PHONE_NUMBER].

## 5. Compatibility by brand and MCC (AFT only)

The availability of AFT depends on the combination of **card brand + merchant MCC**.

### Visa

- **ACCOUNT_TO_ACCOUNT**: MCC `4829`, `6012`, `6051`, `6211`, `6540`
- **PERSON_TO_PERSON**: MCC `4829`

### Mastercard

- **ACCOUNT_TO_ACCOUNT**: MCC `6012`, `6051`, `6211`, `6536`, `6537`, `6540`
- **PERSON_TO_PERSON**: MCC `4829`, `6536`, `6537`, `6538`, `6540`

## 6. Technical integration flow

### 6.1 CFT flow (card credit)

1. **Create a payment order (`POST /payment`)** with `PAYOUT` operation:

```json
{
  "signature": "<signature>",
  "amount": 1500,
  "currency": "978",
  "operative": "PAYOUT",
  "secure": false,
  "customer_ext_id": "user-123",
  "service": "<SERVICE_UUID>",
  "description": "CFT Payout example",
  "url_post": "https://yourstore.com/notification",
  "extra_data": {
    "profile": {
      "first_name": "MarĂ­a",
      "last_name": "Customer",
      "email": "maria@example.com",
      "phone": {
        "number": "600000000",
        "prefix": "34"
      }
    }
  }
}
```

2. **Make the credit (`POST /payment/payout`)** specifying `order_uuid` and `source_uuid`:

```json
{
  "signature": "<signature>",
  "order_uuid": "<ORDER_UUID>",
  "source_uuid": "<CARD_UUID>",
  "customer_ip": "127.0.0.1"
}
```

3. An immediate response is received + notification to the `url_post`.

4. Important: **CFT credits cannot be reversed**.

---

### 6.2 AFT flow (funding from card)

1. **Create a payment order (`POST /payment`)** including `profile`, `billing_address`, and optionally `aft`:

```json
{
  "signature": "<signature>",
  "amount": 2000,
  "currency": "978",
  "operative": "PAYMENT",
  "service": "<SERVICE_UUID>",
  "description": "Account funding via card",
  "url_post": "https://yourstore.com/notification",
  "extra_data": {
    "profile": {
      "first_name": "Luis",
    "last_name": "Customer",
    "email": "luis@example.com",
    "birthdate": "1990-01-01"
  },
  "billing_address": {
    "country": "ESP",
    "address1": "123 Example Street",
    "city": "CastellĂłn",
      "zip_code": "12001",
      "state_code": "CT"
    },
    "aft": {
      "type": "ACCOUNT_TO_ACCOUNT"
    }
  }
}
```

2. Paylands internally decides whether to apply AFT and what type, based on:
   - Merchant configuration  
   - MCC  
   - Card brand  
   - `extra_data.aft.type`

3. An immediate response is received + notification to the `url_post`.

## 7. Summary comparison table

| Feature                                | CFT                                                  | AFT                                                                 |
|----------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Direction of funds                     | Merchant â†’ user's card                               | User's card â†’ own account or another person                         |
| Main operation                         | `PAYOUT` + `/payment/payout`                         | `PAYMENT` + AFT logic in `extra_data.aft`                           |
| Specific endpoint                      | Yes, `POST /payment/payout`                          | No additional (everything in `/payment`)                            |
| Key field                              | `operative: "PAYOUT"`, `secure: false`               | `extra_data.aft.type`                                               |
| Minimum user data                      | Basic `profile`                                      | `profile` + `billing_address` + `receiver` (if applicable)          |
| MCC compatibility                      | Not detailed                                         | Complete table by brand and MCC                                     |
| Reversibility                          | Not reversible                                       | Depends on the flow and refund policies                             |
| Typical scenario                       | Cashout, refunds, payouts                            | Account funding, P2P, top-ups                                       |

## 8. When to use CFT and when to use AFT?

### Use **CFT** when:
- You want to **send money to the user's card**.  
- You need a **direct cashout**.  
- You want an immediate and irreversible credit.  

### Use **AFT** when:
- You need to **load an account** (wallet, e-wallet, prepaid) from a card.  
- You want to enable **P2P transfers**.  
- You require a flow conditioned by MCC, brand, or acquirer rules.
